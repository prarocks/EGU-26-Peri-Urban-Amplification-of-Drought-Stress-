01 / Introduction

Why monsoon drought matters for growing cities

This presentation organizes the analysis of monsoon-season drought conditions and their relationship with urban heat exposure in Kathmandu Valley. The work connects hydroclimatic drought indicators, urban expansion, and settlement-level vulnerability to support clearer planning decisions.

The page is designed as a permanent, shareable web archive for the EGU presentation so collaborators, reviewers, and interested readers can access the figures and interactive outputs directly from GitHub.

05 / Discussion

Interpreting drought-heat relationships in an urbanizing valley

The discussion links the analytical findings with the broader urban climate context. The key interpretation is that monsoon drought should not be treated only as a rainfall anomaly; in dense and expanding urban landscapes it can amplify heat exposure, water stress, and uneven vulnerability.

  • Drought years provide a useful lens for comparing climate stress with urban land-surface response.
  • Spatial patterns help identify where planning, green infrastructure, and water management interventions may be most urgent.
  • Dashboard-based presentation makes the analysis easier to inspect, update, and share beyond the conference poster.
06 / Policy Implementations

Planning responses for heat and drought resilience

Policy implementation should connect drought monitoring with urban planning decisions, especially where rapid land-cover change increases surface heat exposure and water stress.

  • Integrate drought indicators into municipal heat-risk and water-security planning.
  • Prioritize urban greening, permeable surfaces, and open-space protection in high-exposure neighborhoods.
  • Use spatial dashboards as living decision-support tools for agencies, researchers, and local planners.
